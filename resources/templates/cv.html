Research Experience
2012 to Current; Postdoctoral Work
University of California, Berkeley, CA with Professor John F. Hartwig
  • Investigating ruthenium- and palladium-catalyzed oxidative olefination of aromatic carbon-hydrogen bonds for steric-driven regioselective synthesis of styrene and divinylbenzene derivatives.
  • Explored palladium-catalyzed oxidative amination reaction for direct functionalization of aromatic carbon-hydrogen bonds to form protected aniline products with regioselectivities guided by steric effects over electronic effects. The project involved thoroughly examining multiple reaction variables using high-throughput experimentation and implementing a strategy to overcome the problem of incomplete conversion of substrates. The study led to the development of the first method for amination of mono-, di-, and tri-substituted arenes with good yields and excellent regioselectivities from steric effects.
2008 to 2012; Graduate Work
University of Rochester, Rochester, NY with Professor Daniel J. Weix
  • Discovered nickel-catalyzed reductive conjugate addition of primary, secondary and tertiary haloalkanes and functionalized haloarenes to α,β-unsaturated ketones, aldehydes and esters. The discovery provides an alternative, easy-to-perform strategy for the formation of synthetically useful silyl enol ethers.
  • Conducted stoichiometric studies on nickel-catalyzed reductive conjugate addition of haloalkanes to α,β-unsaturated ketones. The study involved the synthesis of (pyridine)Ni(η3-1-triethylsilyloxyalkenyl)Cl complex and allowed us to eliminate three of the four potential mechanisms.
  • Initiated nickel-catalyzed reductive coupling reactions for carbon-carbon bond formation: 1) cross-coupling of haloalkanes with haloarenes for the synthesis of alkylated aromatics, 2) intramolecular coupling of haloalkanes with haloarenes for the formation of 5- and 6-membered rings, 3) allylation of aldehydes and ketones, 4) α-arylation and alkylation of α-haloketones. These studies demonstrated the feasibility of reductive coupling reactions.
  • Conducted stoichiometric studies on nickel-catalyzed reductive cross-coupling reactions of haloalkanes with haloarenes and studied individual effects of ligands used in the reaction. These studies improved our mechanistic understanding of reactions listed above.
  • Examined reductive elimination of heteroatoms (S-O and S-N)from transition metals and performed optimization experiments screening reaction parameters including ligands, temperature and solvents. The investigation led to a radical-mediated stoichiometric copper reaction for the formation of sulfonate esters under mild reaction conditions.
  • Synthesized palladium-sulfinate complexes with bidentate phosphine ligands [(P,P)Pd(SO2p-tol)Cl] and conducted NMR studies of their sulfinyl-O and sulfinyl-N reductive elimination upon addition of alkoxides and amides respectively.
2006-2007; Undergraduate Work
Lake Forest College, Lake Forest, IL with Professor William B. Martin
  • Investigated Sonogashira coupling between regioisomers of iodoanilines and propargylamines (N-protected and unprotected) as a possible synthetic pathway for preparation of kynuramine and its regioisomers. Conducted optimization experiments screening key reaction parameters including palladium catalysts, solvents and reaction temperatures. Demonstrated that cross-coupling worked efficiently with the ortho-isomer compared to the meta-isomer, while no coupling was observed with the para-isomer.
2004-2005; Undergraduate Work
Lake Forest College, Lake Forest, IL with Professor. Shubhik DebBurman
  • Investigated cell biology of Parkinson's disease in a yeast model. Evaluated trehalose dosage effects, via deletion of cellular trehalose level regulating enzymes (trehalose synthase, trehalase N and trehalase A), on α-synuclein localization, misfolding and toxicity in yeast. Demonstrated that deletion of trehalose synthase or trehalase A did not affect α-synuclein localization, misfolding or toxicity.
